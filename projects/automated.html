Automated



Research | Storyboarding | Graphic Design | Animation
Game Design and Development | User Testing

The goal of this project was to design a creative teaching tool to foster better understanding of an engineering psychology subject. I chose to create a side-scrolling video game in order to make use of gamification (using game techniques for a non-game context) concepts to help users perceive and understand the effects of automation on human performance. The learning objectives were that users would be able to understand the levels of automation taxonomy, identify common issues for human performance when automation is involved, and develop strategies to implement correct levels of automation for various situation in order to complete a given task. I selected gamification for my method of delivery for this concept because gamification has been shown to increase motivation, provide entertainment, and shape use behavior.

In this game, the player has control over a human and a robot character. The player is responsible for moving the human character through the level at all times. The robot can be switched from being completely controlled by the player, to being an AI of the game as set by the level of automation. The goal of the player is to get both characters to the end of the level..
